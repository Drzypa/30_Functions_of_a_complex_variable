\begin{thmplain}
\, \, If the infinite product
$$\prod_{\nu=1}^\infty (1\!+\!c_\nu) = (1\!+\!c_1)(1\!+\!c_2)\cdots$$
of complex numbers $1\!+\!c_\nu$ is \PMlinkname{absolutely convergent}{AbsoluteConvergenceOfInfiniteProduct}, then its {\em value}, i.e.\, $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}\prod_{\nu=1}^n (1\!+\!c_\nu)$, does not depend on the \PMlinkescapetext{order of its factors and vanishes only when some factor} is zero.
\end{thmplain}
