Plemelj formulas


Let ψ⁢(ζ) be a density function of a complex variable satisfying the Hölder condition (the Lipschitz condition of order α)11A function f⁢(ζ) satisfies the Hölder condition on a smooth curve C if for every ζ1,ζ2∈C |f⁢(ζ2)-f⁢(ζ1)|≤M⁢|ζ2-ζ1|α, M>0, 0<α≤1. It is clear that the Hölder condition is a weaker restriction than a bounded derivative for f⁢(ζ). on a smooth closed contour C in the integral

Ψ⁢(z)=12⁢π⁢i⁢∫Cψ⁢(ζ)ζ-z⁢𝑑ζ, (1)

then the limits Ψ+⁢(t) and Ψ-⁢(t) as z approaches an arbitrary point t on C from the interior and the exterior of C, respectively, are

{Ψ+⁢(t)≡12⁢ψ⁢(t)+12⁢π⁢i⁢∫Cψ⁢(ζ)ζ-t⁢𝑑ζ,Ψ-⁢(t)≡-12⁢ψ⁢(t)+12⁢π⁢i⁢∫Cψ⁢(ζ)ζ-t⁢𝑑ζ. (2)

These are the Plemelj[1] formulas 22cf.[2], where restrictions that Plemelj made, were relaxed. and the improper integrals in (2) must be interpreted as Cauchy’s principal values.
