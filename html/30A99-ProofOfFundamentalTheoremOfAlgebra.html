proof of fundamental theorem of algebra


If f⁢(x)∈ℂ⁢[x] let a be a root of f⁢(x) in some extension of ℂ. Let K be a Galois closure of ℂ⁢(a) over ℝ and set G=Gal⁡(K/ℝ). Let H be a Sylow 2-subgroup of G and let L=KH (the fixed field of H in K). By the Fundamental Theorem of Galois Theory we have [L:ℝ]=[G:H], an odd number. We may write L=ℝ⁢(b) for some b∈L, so the minimal polynomial mb,ℝ⁢(x) is irreducible over ℝ and of odd degree. That degree must be 1, and hence L=ℝ, which means that G=H, a 2-group. Thus G1=Gal⁡(K/ℂ) is also a 2-group. If G1≠1 choose G2≤G1 such that [G1:G2]=2, and set M=KG2, so that [M:ℂ]=[G1:G2]=2. But any polynomial of degree 2 over ℂ has roots in ℂ by the quadratic formula, so such a field M cannot exist. This contradiction shows that G1=1. Hence K=ℂ and a∈ℂ, completing the proof.
