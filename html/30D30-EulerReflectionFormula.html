Theorem 1

(Euler Reflection Formula)

Γ⁢(x)⁢Γ⁢(1-x)=πsin⁡(π⁢x)

Proof: We have

1Γ⁢(x)=x⁢eγ⁢x⁢∏n=1∞((1+xn)⁢e-x/n)

and thus

1Γ⁢(x)⁢1Γ⁢(-x)=-x2⁢eγ⁢x⁢e-γ⁢x⁢∏n=1∞((1+xn)⁢e-x/n)⁢((1-xn)⁢ex/n)=-x2⁢∏n=1∞(1-x2n2)

But Γ⁢(1-x)=-x⁢Γ⁢(-x) and thus

1Γ⁢(x)⁢1Γ⁢(1-x)=x⁢∏n=1∞(1-x2n2)

Now, using the formula (http://planetmath.org/ExamplesOfInfiniteProducts) for sin⁡x/x, we have

sin⁡(π⁢x)=π⁢x⁢∏n=1∞(1-x2n2)

so that

1Γ⁢(x)⁢1Γ⁢(1-x)=sin⁡(π⁢x)π

and the result follows.
