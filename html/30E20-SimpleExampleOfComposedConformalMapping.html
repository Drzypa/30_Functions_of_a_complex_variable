simple example of composed conformal mapping


Let’s consider the mapping

f:ℂ→ℂ with f⁢(z)=a⁢z+b,

where a and b are complex and  a≠0.

Because  f′⁢(z)≡a≠0,  the mapping is conformal in the whole z-plane.  Denote  a:=ϱ⁢ei⁢α (where  ϱ,α∈ℝ) and

z1:=ϱ⁢z, (1)
z2:=ei⁢α⁢z1, (2)
w:=z2+b. (3)

Then the mapping  z↦z1  means a dilation in the complex plane, the mapping  z1↦z2  a rotation by the angle α and the mapping  z2↦w  a translation determined by the vector from the origin to the point b.  Thus f is composed of these three consecutive mappings which all are conformal.
