evaluating the gamma function at 1/2


In the entry on the gamma function it is mentioned that Γ⁢(1/2)=π. In this entry we reduce the proof of this claim to the problem of computing the area under the bell curve. First note that by definition of the gamma function,

Γ⁢(1/2) =∫0∞e-x⁢x-1/2⁢𝑑x
=2⁢∫0∞e-x⁢12⁢x⁢𝑑x.

Performing the substitution u=x, we find that d⁢u=12⁢x⁢d⁢x, so

Γ⁢(1/2)=2⁢∫0∞e-u2⁢𝑑u=∫ℝe-u2⁢𝑑u,

where the last equality holds because e-u2 is an even function. Since the area under the bell curve is π, it follows that Γ⁢(1/2)=π.
