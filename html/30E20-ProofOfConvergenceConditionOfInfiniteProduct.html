proof of convergence condition of infinite product


proof of theorem of convergence of infinite productFernando Sanz Gamiz

Proof.

Let pn=∏i=1nui. We have to study the convergence of the sequence {pn}. The sequence {pn} converges to a not null limit iff {log⁡pn} (log is restricted to its principal branch) converges to a finite limit. By the Cauchy criterion, this happens iff for every ϵ′>0 there exist N such that |log⁡pn+k-log⁡pn|<ϵ′ for all n>N and all k=1,2,…, i.e, iff

|log⁡pn+kpn|=|log⁡un+1⁢un+2⁢⋯⁢un+k|<ϵ′;

as log⁡(z) is an injective function and continuous at z=1 and log⁡(1)=0 this will happen iff for every ϵ>0

|un+1⁢un+2⁢⋯⁢un+k-1|<ϵ

for n greater than N and k=1,2,… ∎
