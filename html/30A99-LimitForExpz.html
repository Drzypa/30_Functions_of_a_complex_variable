limit for exp(z)


For any complex number z, we have

limn→∞⁡(1+zn+o⁢(1n))n=exp⁡z,

where exp denotes the exponential function.
Proof: For α→0, we have

ln⁡(1+α) =∑k=1∞(-1)k-1⋅αkk
=α+O⁢(α2).

Therefore

(1+zn+o⁢(1n))n = exp⁡(n⁢ln⁡(1+zn+o⁢(1n)))
= exp⁡(n⁢(zn+o⁢(1n)+O⁢(1n2)))
= exp(z+o(1)+O(1n))→expz for n→∞. □
