generalized Cauchy integral formula


Theorem.

Let U⊂C be a domain with C1 boundary. Let f:U→C be a C1 function that is C1 up to the boundary. Then for z∈U,

f⁢(z)=12⁢π⁢i⁢∫∂⁡Uf⁢(w)w-z⁢𝑑w-12⁢π⁢i⁢∫U∂⁡f∂⁡z¯⁢(w)w-z⁢𝑑w¯∧d⁢w.

Note that C1 up to the boundary means that the function and the derivative extend to be continuous functions on the closure of U. The theorem follows from Stokes’ theorem. When f is holomorphic, then the second term is zero and this is the classical Cauchy integral formula.
