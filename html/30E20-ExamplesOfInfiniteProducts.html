examples of infinite products


A classic example is the Riemann zeta function. For ℜ⁡(z)>1 we have

ζ⁢(z)=∑n=1∞1nz=∏p⁢ prime11-p-z.

where z is an arbitrary complex number. Taking the logarithmic derivative (a frequent move in connection with infinite products) we get a decomposition of the cotangent into partial fractions:

π⁢cot⁡π⁢z=1z+∑n=1∞(1z+n+1z-n). (2)

The equation (2), in turn, has some interesting uses, e.g. to get the Taylor expansion of an Eisenstein series, or to evaluate ζ⁢(2⁢n) for positive integers n.
