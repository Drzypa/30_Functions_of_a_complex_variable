proof of limit rule of product


Let f and g be real (http://planetmath.org/RealFunction) or complex functions having the limits

limx→x0⁡f⁢(x)=F and limx→x0⁡g⁢(x)=G.

Then also the limit limx→x0⁡f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x) exists and equals F⁢G.

Proof.  Let ε be any positive number.  The assumptions imply the existence of the positive numbers δ1,δ2,δ3 such that

|f⁢(x)-F|<ε2⁢(1+|G|)⁢when⁢  0<|x-x0|<δ1 (1)
|g⁢(x)-G|<ε2⁢(1+|F|)⁢when⁢  0<|x-x0|<δ2, (2)
|g⁢(x)-G|<1⁢when⁢  0<|x-x0|<δ3. (3)

According to the condition (3) we see that

|g⁢(x)|=|g⁢(x)-G+G|≦|g⁢(x)-G|+|G|<1+|G|⁢when⁢  0<|x-x0|<δ3.

Supposing then that  0<|x-x0|<min⁡{δ1,δ2,δ3}  and using (1) and (2) we obtain

|f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x)-F⁢G| =|f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x)-F⁢g⁢(x)+F⁢g⁢(x)-F⁢G|
≦|f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x)-F⁢g⁢(x)|+|F⁢g⁢(x)-F⁢G|
=|g⁢(x)|⋅|f⁢(x)-F|+|F|⋅|g⁢(x)-G|
<(1+|G|)⁢ε2⁢(1+|G|)+(1+|F|)⁢ε2⁢(1+|F|)
=ε

This settles the proof.
