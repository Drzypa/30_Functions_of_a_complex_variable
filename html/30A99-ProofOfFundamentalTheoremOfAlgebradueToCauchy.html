proof of fundamental theorem of algebra (due to Cauchy)


We will prove that any equation

f⁢(z):=zn+a1⁢zn-1+a2⁢zn-2+…+an-1⁢z+an= 0,

where the coefficients aj are complex numbers and  n≧1,  has at least one root (http://planetmath.org/Equation) in ℂ.

Proof.  We can suppose that  an≠0.  Denote  z:=x+i⁢y  where  x,y are real.  Then the function

g⁢(x,y):=|f⁢(z)|=|f⁢(x+i⁢y)|

is defined and continuous in the whole ℝ2.  Let  c:=∑j=1n|aj|; it is positive.  Using the triangle inequality we make the estimation

|f⁢(z)|  =|z|n⁢|1+a1z+a2z2+…+anzn|
 ≧(1-|a1||z|-|a2||z|2+…-|an||z|n)
 ≧(1-|a1||z|-|a2||z|+…-|an||z|)
 =|z|n⁢(1-c|z|)≧12⁢|z|n,

being true for  |z|>max⁡{1, 2⁢c}.  Denote  r:=max⁡{1, 2⁢c,2⁢|an|n}.  Consider the disk  x2+y2≦r2.  Because it is compact, the function  g⁢(x,y)  attains at a point  (x0,y0)  of the disk its absolute minimum value (infimum) in the disk.  If  |z|>r,  we have

g⁢(x,y)=|f⁢(z)|≧12⁢|z|n>12⁢rn≧12⁢(2⁢|an|n)n=|an|> 0.

Thus

g⁢(x0,y0)≦g⁢(0, 0)=|an|<|f⁢(z)| for⁢|z|>r.

Hence g⁢(x0,y0) is the absolute minimum of g⁢(x,y) in the whole complex plane.  We show that  g⁢(x0,y0)=0.  Therefore we make the antithesis that  g⁢(x0,y0)>0.

Denote  z0:=x0+i⁢y0,   z:=z0+u  and

f⁢(z)=f⁢(z0+u):=bn+bn-1⁢u+bn-2⁢u2+…+b1⁢un-1+un.

Then  bn=f⁢(z0)≠0  by the antithesis.  Moreover, denote

cj:=bjbn (j= 1, 2,…,n),c0:=1bn.

and assume that  cn-1=cn-2=…=cn-k+1=0  but  cn-k≠0.  Thus we may write

f⁢(z)=bn⁢(1+cn-k⁢uk+cn-k-1⁢uk+1+…+c0⁢un).

If  cn-k=p⁢(cos⁡α+i⁢sin⁡α)  and  u=ϱ⁢(cos⁡φ+i⁢sin⁡φ), then

cn-k⁢uk=p⁢ϱk⁢[cos⁡(α+k⁢φ)+i⁢sin⁡(α+k⁢φ)]

by de Moivre identity.  Choosing  ϱ≦1  and  φ=π-αk  we get  cn-k⁢uk=-p⁢ϱk  and can make the estimation

|cn-k-1⁢uk+1+…+c0⁢un⏟h⁢(u)|≦|cn-k-1|⁢ϱk+1+…+|c0|⁢ϱn≦(|cn-k-1|+…+|c0|)⁢ϱk+1:=R⁢ϱk+1

where R is a constant.  Let now  ϱ=min⁡{1,1pk,p2⁢R}.  We obtain

|f⁢(z)|  =|bn|⋅|1-p⁢ϱk+h⁢(u)|
 ≦|bn|⁢[|1-p⁢ϱk|+|h⁢(u)|]
 ≦|bn|⁢[1-p⁢ϱk+R⁢ϱk+1]
 ≦|bn|⁢[1-ϱk⁢(p-R⁢ϱ)]
 ≦|bn|⁢[1-ϱk⁢(p-R⋅p2⁢R)]
 ≦|bn|⁢[1-1p⋅p2]
 ≦|bn|2<|bn|=|f⁢(z0)|,

which result is impossible since |f(z0| was the absolute minimum.  Consequently, the antithesis is wrong, and the proof is settled.
